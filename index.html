Pet friendly plants

Click here for the HTA Guide to potentially harmful plants to check if what is already in your garden is safe for your pets.

There are several outdoor plants that are considered pet-friendly and safe for your furry friends.

Here are a few examples:

  • Marigolds (Tagetes): These vibrant flowers are safe for pets and can add a pop of color to your outdoor space.
  • Boston Fern (Nephrolepis exaltata): This lush fern is non-toxic to pets and can thrive in shady areas.
  • Spider Plant (Chlorophytum comosum): Spider plants are safe for cats and dogs and are known for their air-purifying qualities.
  • African Violet (Saintpaulia): These small, flowering plants are non-toxic to pets and can be a lovely addition to your outdoor garden or patio.
  • Rosemary (Rosmarinus officinalis): Rosemary is an aromatic herb that is safe for pets and can be grown in containers or in the ground.
  • Catnip (Nepeta cataria): Catnip is a plant that cats are particularly fond of, and it is safe for them to interact with. However, be aware that it may attract cats from the neighborhood.
  • Sunflowers (Helianthus): Sunflowers are pet-friendly and can create a cheerful and vibrant atmosphere in your outdoor space.
  • Zinnias (Zinnia elegans): These colorful annual flowers are safe for pets and can be a great addition to your garden or flower beds.
Remember that while these plants are generally considered safe for pets, individual pets may react differently to certain plants. It's always a good idea to observe your pets around new plants and consult with a veterinarian if you have any concerns about specific plants and your pet's well-being.
