Cacti & Succulents

Cacti and succulents are the perfect companions for your home! Not only are they incredibly low maintenance and able to survive in dry conditions, but they also add a touch of charm to any room. You'll be delighted to know that some of these lovely plants can even thrive outdoors, as long as they soak up plenty of sunshine and have well-drained soil. With a wide variety to choose from, you'll easily find these affordable treasures, making them a fun and exciting addition to your plant collection. Get ready to be amazed and fall in love with these fascinating beauties!

Looks

You can easily spot cacti and succulents thanks to their unique features like plump leaves or stems designed to store water. These incredible plants come in a wide array of shapes, colors, and forms that will leave you awe-struck. From spiky varieties to smooth or fuzzy ones, and from softly rounded to boldly angular and sculptural forms, there's something for everyone's taste.

Light & Ventilation

Cacti and succulents typically thrive in environments with low moisture, dry air, excellent drainage, and warm temperatures. However, it's worth noting that certain varieties can still flourish in partially shaded areas with a bit of humidity.

Colder temperatures increase the risk of rot, so it's important to protect them during chilly spells. Most of these resilient plants are sun-lovers and prefer abundant sunlight near windows, so it's best to provide them with ample natural light to keep them happy and healthy.

Watering & Feeding

Let's debunk a common misconception about cacti and succulents: while they possess remarkable water-storing abilities in their leaves and stems, it's incorrect to assume that they only need minimal water. These resilient plants won't thrive on scarcity alone. Adequate watering plays a crucial role in their growth and well-being. However, finding the right balance is key. Overwatering will impede their growth, while underwatering leads to shriveling. Here are some guidelines to keep in mind throughout the seasons:

Spring & Summer

During the active growing season, it's recommended to water your plants at least once a week. When watering, ensure that the soil receives a thorough soaking, allowing any excess water to drain away. Allow the potting mix to slightly dry out between waterings.

Additionally, feeding your plants once a month promotes healthy growth, enhances disease tolerance, and encourages beautiful flowering. Autumn

Autumn & Winter

This is the period when your plants enter a rest phase. Reduce watering, allowing the potting mix to dry out between waterings. The frequency of watering depends on the specific environment and the type of succulent you have. Winter-flowering cacti may require regular watering while being kept in warm conditions, whereas desert-dwelling varieties can be left without water during this time. You do not need to feed cacti or succulents during the rest period.

Some cacti and succulents have a lifespan of up to 300 years!
